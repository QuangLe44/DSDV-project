About The Project

Electricity is one of if not the most important source of energy to operate the world. The discovery of electricity is truly a revolution to the world, and from that many inventions been made.

The project aims to provide a thorough and aesthetically appealing analysis of worldwide electricity statistics concerning installed capacity, distribution loss, net generation, and net consumption. This will aid in improving comprehension of the dynamic global energy consumption and infrastructure development landscape.

Downside
Energy Loss during Transmission
Climate Change
Energy losses can occur during the distribution and transmission of power across large distances. A portion of the electricity is lost as heat due to resistance in power lines and transformers, which lowers overall efficiency.
Higher net consumption means higher more changes to the climate but in a negative way. Emissions of greenhouse gases, air pollutants, and the environmental impact of resource extraction for electricity generation pose significant challenges.
